## What is an accounting flow?

An accounting flow is the rule that decides when and how your hotel's revenue and payments get posted into your accounting system. Omniboost supports three main flows. The choice depends on your country's accounting requirements, your accountant's preference, and the accounting system you use.

## Consumed flow (the most common)

With the Consumed flow, revenue is posted on the day it is earned. If a guest stays for 5 nights at 100 EUR per night, 100 EUR of accommodation revenue is posted every single day of the stay. Payments are also posted on the day they are registered in Mews. This is the most widely used flow because it matches how Mews naturally tracks data in its Accounting Report on a Consumed basis.

## Consumed flow: practical example

A guest stays 5 nights, total 500 EUR. With Consumed flow: 100 EUR revenue is posted on each of the 5 days. The 500 EUR payment is posted on the day the guest pays, for example at checkout on day 5. Your accounting report reconciles using the Mews Accounting Report on Consumed basis.

## Closed (bills) flow: journal entries

With the Closed flow, nothing is posted until a bill or folio is closed, meaning the guest checks out and the bill is finalised. On checkout day, all revenue from the entire stay is posted at once, along with the payments tied to that bill. This is common in countries where accounting regulations require posting on invoice date rather than consumption date.

## Closed (bills and invoices) flow: sales entries

A variation of the Closed flow where individual bills and invoices are posted as sales entries (debtor invoices) rather than journal entries. Each invoice goes into the accounts receivable section of your accounting system with debtor information attached. Payments may or may not be included depending on your configuration.

## Hybrid flow

Some countries such as Spain require a combination of both Closed and Consumed approaches due to local accounting law. Omniboost supports this Hybrid flow where needed.

## End of day

The default extraction window runs from midnight to midnight. If your hotel has a different operational end of day, for example 6:00 AM because late-night bar revenue should land on the same day as the dinner revenue, you can configure a different end of day by contacting your Omniboost representative.
