## Two different types of accounts receivable

In hotel accounting, there are two different groups of people who owe you money: guests who are currently staying (or have just checked out), and companies or individuals who are not registered guests. Each group gets its own ledger account.

## Guest ledger (also called front-office or transient ledger)

The guest ledger holds all the amounts owed by guests who are currently registered and staying at your hotel. When a guest checks in and starts consuming services (rooms, meals, spa, etc.), those charges build up on their account in the guest ledger. When they check out and pay, the guest ledger is cleared.

## City ledger (also called accounts receivable ledger)

The city ledger is for non-registered guests, typically companies that have a credit account with your hotel. For example, a corporate client whose employees stay regularly and are billed monthly. The charges go to the city ledger, and the company pays an invoice later. City ledger accounts are essentially debtors in your accounting system.

## How Omniboost handles these

When Omniboost transfers your daily revenue and payments, it needs to know the correct ledger account codes for both the guest ledger and the city ledger. When guests check out and pay immediately, the guest ledger balance clears. When a city ledger invoice is issued, it goes to accounts receivable in your accounting system and stays there until the company pays.

## What you need to provide

During onboarding, you will need to provide Omniboost with the ledger account code for both your guest ledger and your city ledger. These should already exist in your Chart of Accounts. If they do not, create them in your accounting system first.
