## What is a fallback account?

A fallback account is a safety net. Sometimes a revenue or payment category in Mews does not have a ledger account code assigned to it, either it was added recently and nobody mapped it yet, or the mapping was deleted by mistake. Without a fallback, Omniboost would fail to post that entry and you would have a gap in your accounting. With a fallback, Omniboost posts the entry to the fallback account instead, so the total still balances. The amount is just in the wrong place temporarily.

## You should have two fallback accounts

Omniboost recommends creating at least two separate fallback accounts: one for revenue (so unmapped revenue lands in the right category), and one for payments (so unmapped payments land in the right category). If you mix them into one account, it is harder to tell what went wrong.

## What to do when you see amounts on fallback accounts

If you notice amounts appearing on your fallback accounts in your accounting system, it means Omniboost could not find a mapping for those entries. Go to your mapping configuration in the Omniboost portal and find which categories are missing their account codes. Once you assign the correct codes, future entries will go to the right place. For past entries that landed in the fallback, you may need to make a manual correction in your accounting system.

## Fallback accounts vs. suspense accounts

A fallback account catches unmapped categories. A suspense account is used intentionally for payments: it is a temporary holding account for a payment that has been registered in Mews but not yet cleared by the bank. Both exist for different reasons.
